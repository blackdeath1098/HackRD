More info. on holidays celebrated in Brazil

February,Carnival Festivals

These carnival festivals attracts tourists every year. Some important and memorable carnival festivals include Rio de Janeiro, the Carnival of Olina and Recife. Rio de Janeiro, in particular samba schools compete to be the best every year.

October 12,The Lady of Aparecida Celebration

Every year on October 12, about 170,000 people visit the sanctuary of the Lady of Aparecida.Aparecida means "appeared", built in 1960's largest sanctuary in the world.

April 15,Festivals of the Cavalhadas in Pirenópolis (Goiás)

A national Holiday, this holiday's purpose is to call attention to the battles in which the Christians drove out the Moors

April 21, Festival of Tirandentes

A national holiday, where Brazilians commemorates the death of Joaquin Jose da Silva Xavier, he was known as a hero for fighting independence for Brazil.

March-April, Easter and holy week

Brazilians take to the streets, showing the religious passion of the country home to the largest number of Catholics in the world.

September 7, Independence Day

Brazilians honor Don Pedro l, which declared the seperation of the country from Portugal, officially declared it's own country in 1822.
